02/20/2009

Friends of our beloved CornShark, I come bearing news: The absolute best version of the game to date is now available for download. Right now. Right this millisecond. Go get it!

Now, I know you're gonna say, "Yeah, we've heard this before." But this time it really is the very best version ever, and without going into the mind-numbing technical details, I'll tell ya why:

  • The balance has been adjusted. Now there are no wasted points. I'm sure you've all faced the irritation and disappointment that happens when you're only four points from leveling up, you eat a Whalemelon, and only get the four points needed to level up. All the other points get wasted. Well, not anymore. Now if there are any points in excess of what is needed to level up, those points get applied to the next level. (You're welcome.)
  • The game has been upgraded to use the new MHFramework 2.0 engine. What does that mean? Without going into technical details, it means the software under the hood runs a lot smoother, has fewer lags, doesn't spew hideous error messages all over the console screen, and behaves equally well in both windowed and full-screen modes.
01/30/2009

Greetings, CornShark fans!

If you're like me, there's nothing you'd rather do on Super Bowl Sunday than lock yourself away and play video games. You're in luck, my friends, because right now, Super Bowl Weekend, there is a new version of CornShark available for download. It has all the fish-munching action you've grown to love, plus the following improvements:

  • A higher-contrast background texture for people with dim monitors. (So stop whining about not seeing any movement, will ya?) Seriously, I want your opinion on it. I kinda like it, Michelle doesn't, so tell me what you think.
  • No more bonus for eating large fish. The bonus didn't help that much anyway. However, there is still the point adjustment for beating up on smaller fish. I think it seems fair and balanced now.
  • Fixed the bug that happened when there was a lot of biting onscreen. Now the CornShark stands a fighting chance even when he's overwhelmed in "school". (Get it?)
  • Fixed the fish hook bug where the hook icon was appearing even when another fish ate the bait.
  • Difficulty adjustment on the Easy skill level. The game is attracting quite a number of young fans, and the game is just too difficult for some of them. So now when you choose the Easy level on the Options Screen, the CornShark has a 50% stronger bite and the other fish have a 25% weaker one. Also, the fishing line on the bait is more easily visible.
  • Minor performance optimizations on collision processing system. Simpler logic and fewer unnecessary operations.

06/06/2008

First of all, an appreciative shout-out to my testers! I know some of you feel like you haven't done anything since very few problems were discovered, but you've still done the job. So thank you, thank you, thank you!

Now, after a number of delays (one being that my laptop bit the big one; another being that early versions of Windows Vista are unforgivable pieces of garbage), the CornShark project has once again started crawling along, and now Alpha 2 is under development. If my volunteer testers don't find any major defects in this version, then I'll consider the Alpha phase to be successfully completed. And then it's on to the Beta, where things get MUCH more exciting!

Here are my responses to some of the comments that were made during testing. Please forgive me for paraphrasing and/or summarizing in some cases.

"After a fish bites me, he just swims around me like crazy." That's the AI glitch I was talking about before. I thought it was fixed, but it still exists and it's making me crazy! I haven't given up, though. It's not gonna get me without a fight!
"There's no video when I run it under Windows Vista." Vista doesn't seem to like the active rendering algorithm that MHFramework uses in full-screen mode, so I modified some of the core framework classes to give users the option of running in a windowed mode. You'll see it in Alpha 2, which will hopefully be available shortly.
"Timed powerups expire when you pause the game." That's because they're measured in real time and the program doesn't compensate for the pause. I might fix this, but I'm not really that concerned about it. Powerups are plentiful, so you can always grab another one. Otherwise, just don't pause the game while you're under the influence of a timed powerup. They only last a few seconds anyway -- fifteen at most.
"The game would have a better feel if the 'up' animation were more fluid." You're probably right. I'll play with that and see what I can do. Still, I've had positive feedback regarding the simplicity and responsivness of the controls as they are, so if I can't smooth it out effectively, it'll still be OK.
"The game is too easy." I know, but I'm intentionally refraining from any gameplay balancing until I have the other fish types in there. The challenge level will change when they are present. If the game is still too easy, then I'll address the issue.
"The game ends too abruptly." Agreed. In the Beta, I'm going to add some sort of endgame animation to amplify the players' feelings of victory/defeat.
"You should keep score or something to make it more competitive." I'm a step ahead of you, my friends. I was already planning to implement a timer in the game so it would keep track of the fastest victory times, then maintain that information in a "high scores" list similar to the one used by MO Techtris.
"Is that really the audio you're going to use?" No! Not at all! The sounds are just files that I happened to have on my laptop at the time. They're just standing in until I can create the "real" sounds.

And the music? It's from Super Mario Brothers, The Little Mermaid, and Lemmings. It's also just standing in until my multitalented bro-in-law Kevin writes something exclusively for this game.

03/05/2008

CornShark goes Alpha!

That's right. The coolest game character since Mario* is getting closer to greeting the public with his corny yet charismatic grin. And you, my dear friends, are among the first to be offered this once-in-a-lifetime** opportunity to take part in shaping the final product.

In case y'all are wonderin'...an Alpha version is one that contains all of the core gameplay features, while still lacking various elements of its content. For this game, that means:

  • The player has all of his abilities. He can swim in all directions, attack his prey, get bit, gain and lose levels, and die.
  • Some animations are in place, but not most of them, and some need a little tweaking.
  • The power-ups are all implemented.
  • The game options are all working.
  • Several basic AI behaviors are present, though they're not perfect by any means.
  • There are placeholders for the sound and music, but not the sounds or songs that will be used in the final version.

The next phase of the project is the Beta phase, which will include:

  • Several more character types. (For a complete list of what's to come, visit http://www.michaeljhenson.info/cornshark.html.)***
  • A lot more animation
  • The actual sound and music made specifically for this game (thanks to Michelle and Kevin for helping there!)
  • A title screen, credits, shameless plugs, and so on.
  • More important testing, 'cause it'll identify the final required tasks.

02/04/2008

Being snowed in at home this past weekend had some serious advantages...

CornShark is now playable! In this case, "playable" means you can swim around the game world and munch on (and get munched by) the few fish that are currently in the game. You can also take damage, earn points, level up, and die (though not necessarily in that order).

The Crackerfishy and the Rainbow Slush Trout are completed in their entirety. The base image of the Pearahna is also swimming around in there, but it's neither finished nor animated yet. All of the player behaviors and abilities are working, and the user interface is complete aside from a power-up timer.

Here are the tasks remaining for the Alpha version. Thankfully, most of them are small.

  • The skill level system needs to be refactored and the affected classes updated.
  • Endgame conditions must be checked and acknowledged. Right now, the game just keeps going when it should be over.
  • Power-ups must be implemented. Not necessarily the graphic designs, but the functionality at least.
  • The hunting, fleeing, and attacking AI behaviors need to be present in some basic form. The fish sometimes appear to be moving intelligently now, but it's all random and coincidental.
  • Basic sound and music need to be added, mostly for testing purposes at this point. I'll probably just grab some random sound and MIDI files to fill in until I can turn my attention to those tasks.

Then it's on to the Beta version, where I finish all the fish designs, special effects, sounds, scenery animations, play balancing, etc, etc.
