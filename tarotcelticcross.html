Tarot Spread: The Celtic Cross

How to Use This Program

The Setup

  1. Make sure JavaScript in enabled in your browser!
  2. Get a deck of Tarot cards. Any deck will do.

The Procedure

  1. Shuffle the cards thoroughly. Try to use a shuffling technique that will not damage the edges of your cards.
  2. Decide on a subject for the spread. It can be a question, person, situation, event, thing, ect.
  3. Draw ten cards, one at a time. For each card that you draw, select it from the appropriate dropdown list in the lookup form. The first card that you draw will be selected from the list labeled "1. The Present", the second will be selected from "2. The Immediate Challenge", and so on.
    If you'd like, you may lay them out in the Celtic Cross pattern shown at the bottom of this page.
  4. When you have drawn all ten cards and selected them in the lookup form, click the button below the form to generate a report explaining the meaning of each card.

Notable Differences Between Tarot Decks

  • Disks vs. Pentacles: Some Tarot decks have a suit of Pentacles while others have a suit of Disks instead. This program uses Disks, so Queen of Disks = Queen of Pentacles, and so on.
  • Highpriest vs. Heirophant: In some decks, Major Arcana card V is The Heirophant, and in others it is The Highpriest. These are assumed equivalent.

The Lookup Form

Select the cards you have drawn from the dropdown lists below. Order is important, so pay attention to the numbers on the form.
